Astro Pi Project: Energy saving counts!

The project has been carried out within the
European Astro Pi Challenge promoted by ESA (European Space Agency).
The aim of the project is to investigate how environmental parameters
such as temperature, pressure, humidity in the Columbus Module are regulated to optimize
comfort and wellness conditions of the astronauts complying energy-saving requirements.
A small single-board computer and its sensors, Sense Hat, are used to acquire data.

    The program will acquire the update values of temperature, humidity and pressure on board of the ISS.
    A camera, used as a sensor will detect, throught facial recognition, if an astronaut is present in the module.
    This will allow to check possible changes of the environmental parameters depending on the presence
    of astronauts for energy saving issue. If there are not astronauts in the module a Super Mario animation
    will be shown on the LED matrix of the Sense Hat. Otherwise the acquired data will be displayed.
